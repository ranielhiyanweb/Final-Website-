BIOGRAPHY

The Early Years (1853 - 1880)

Born in the Netherlands, van Gogh worked as an art dealer and missionary before devoting himself fully to painting. Struggling with poverty and mental health, he found solace in creating art that captured the raw beauty of nature and human emotion.

The Paris Period (1886-1888)

In Paris, van Gogh met Impressionist painters like Monet and Renoir, who inspired him to use brighter colors and looser brushstrokes. This marked a major shift in his artistic style.

The Final Chapter (1888-1890)

In Arles and Saint-Rémy, van Gogh created his most famous works – including 'The Starry Night' and 'Sunflowers' – before his tragic death at 37. Today, he is celebrated as one of the greatest artists of all time.
